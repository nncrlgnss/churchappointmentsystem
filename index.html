ABOUT

  • The Sagrada Família is an iconic basilica in Barcelona designed by Antoni Gaudí, celebrated for its stunning architecture that fuses
    Gothic and Art Nouveau styles and remains under construction since 1882.

    The Sagrada Família was conceived in 1882 by architect Francisco de Paula del Villar, who initially designed it in a traditional Gothic style. However, after a year, Antoni Gaudí took over the project and transformed it with his unique vision, incorporating intricate details, vibrant colors, and symbolic elements. Gaudí devoted the last 15 years of his life solely to the Sagrada Família, blending natural forms and religious themes into his design. The basilica has faced numerous challenges, including the Spanish Civil War, during which many of Gaudí’s original plans and models were destroyed. Despite these setbacks, construction has continued, driven by funding from private donations, with an anticipated completion date set for 2026, marking the centenary of Gaudí's death.

    Unfinished Masterpiece: Construction of the Sagrada Família began in 1882 and is expected to be completed in 2026, more than 140 years after its inception.

    Unique Architecture: The basilica combines elements of Gothic and Art Nouveau architecture, featuring 18 towers representing the Twelve Apostles, the Virgin Mary, the four Evangelists, and Jesus Christ.

    Symbolic Design: Gaudí infused the basilica with Christian symbolism; for example, the Nativity façade represents the birth of Jesus, while the Passion façade depicts his crucifixion.

    UNESCO World Heritage Site: In 2005, the Sagrada Família was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its artistic and cultural significance, recognizing its status as one of the most remarkable architectural achievements of the 20th century.
